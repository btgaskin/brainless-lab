---
title: Concepts — the ladder
description: "The core ontology of BrainlessLab: reservoirs inside composed embodiments, agents inside synchronous ensembles, and worlds that mediate every interaction."
---

BrainlessLab is a **ladder of collectives**. A reservoir is a population of locally
coupled node-like units; an ensemble is a population of embodied agents coupled through a
world. The same principles—local state, explicit interfaces, and synchronous updates—hold
at both scales.

```text
NodeModel → Reservoir → Agent(Reservoir, Embodiment) → Ensemble(Environment) → Run
             ▲ nodes in                              ▲ agents in
               a reservoir                            a world
```

## The rungs

- **Node / `NodeModel`** — the atomic neuron-like unit and its parameters.
- **`Reservoir`** — a population of nodes, its wiring, and its dynamic state. Its central
  contract is `step!(reservoir, receptors)`.
- **`AbstractBody`** — the public dispatch boundary for anything that can mediate between
  a reservoir and a world.
- **`Embodiment <: AbstractBody`** — the generic concrete body. It composes geometry,
  sensors, encoders, actuators, dynamics, physiology, traits, and runtime state; biological
  and robotic organisms differ in component values, not body subclasses.
- **`Agent`** — a reservoir paired with an `AbstractBody`.
- **`Environment`** — owns external state and relations. `TaskEnvironment` adapts the
  existing vector-valued task worlds; `ObjectWorld` is the generic physical world for
  composed embodiments; `SituatedEnvironment` remains the adapter for established
  collective and foraging tasks.
- **`Ensemble`** — one or more agents in an environment, advanced synchronously.
- **Runner / run** — the execution and artifact layer: fixed, plastic, or evolutionary
  evaluation with optional recording.

## Composition, not organism classes

`Embodiment` is intentionally one type. Its component graph is the variation surface:

```text
geometry
sensors → encoders → receptor ports
actuators → commands → dynamics
physiology → feedback receptors, effects, viability
traits + runtime state
```

Every configured component has a stable ID. Receptor and effector ports are namespaced by
the owning component ID, so recording, overrides, and development target identities rather
than tuple positions. `component_slots(body)` exposes the graph without requiring callers to
inspect concrete storage types.

The three checked-in presets illustrate the range without introducing special body types:

- `examples/embodiments/bilateral_insect.toml` — paired field probes, a bilateral contrast
  encoder, unicycle locomotion, and two regulated variables;
- `examples/embodiments/differential_robot.toml` — an RGB-like spectral camera and
  differential drive;
- `examples/embodiments/planar_uav.toml` — spectral vision, paired radio probes, and planar
  force/yaw dynamics.

These names are presets, not claims of high-fidelity organism or vehicle simulation.

## One ensemble, homogeneous or mixed

An `Ensemble` is a population of **1…N** agents. A single-agent task is an ensemble of one;
a dyad and a swarm use the same lifecycle. Homogeneous agents retain a concrete fast path.
Mixed concrete agent/body types are grouped behind function barriers, while stable
`EntityID`s preserve world identity independently of group or slot:

```julia
ids = entity_ids(ensemble)
agent = agent_at_slot(ensemble, 2)

foreach_group(ensemble) do group
    @info group_ids(group) group_slots(group)
end
```

The current runtime is **fixed-population**: identities survive grouping and death, but the
core loop does not yet schedule births, replacement, or lineage transitions.

## The synchronous lifecycle

Each tick separates observation, neural update, world mutation, and physiology:

```text
world sample
  → sensor samples
  → encoder receptor vector + physiology feedback
  → reservoir step and readout
  → actuator decode into typed command
  → dynamics integration / world action
  → environmental effects and exposures
  → physiology update and viability
  → recording by stable entity/component IDs
```

All agents sample the same pre-action world state before commands are applied. An
environment decides **what happened**—contact, resource exposure, collision—while the body
decides **what an effect means** for its physiology. This ownership keeps ecological rules
out of the neural model and internal regulation out of the world.

## Capacities and qualities

The ladder says what the objects are; experiments ask what they can do. A task probes a
capacity such as responsiveness, coordination, adaptation, or memory. Analyses describe
qualities that may support those capacities—criticality, timescale separation, coupling,
and oscillation. Scores are calibrated indicators, not universal measures of cognition.

The commitment is local and self-organising: no global steering term is hidden in the
reservoir or body. Agents couple through what their environment lets them sense and affect.
Physical contact is explicit, and sensor geometry can itself define the interaction
topology.
