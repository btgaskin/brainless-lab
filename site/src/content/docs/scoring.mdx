A task score is an operational measurement under one declared task. It is not the capacity
itself, and it is not sufficient evidence of cognition, intelligence, or biological
validity.

Use `task_outcome(sim)` as the task's declared scalar result:

```julia
outcome = task_outcome(sim)
# (key=:track_score, raw=..., normalized=...)
```

The result is `nothing` when the task declares no scalar outcome. Other metric fields remain
diagnostics unless the task declares them as its outcome.

## Anchor normalisation

A scored `TaskSpec` contains a floor and ceiling. BrainlessLab maps the raw score into their
declared interval:

$$
\text{normalised}
=
\operatorname{clamp}\!\left(
\frac{\text{raw}-\text{floor}}
     {\text{ceiling}-\text{floor}},
0,1
\right).
$$

Read this value as the fraction of that task's anchor interval reached by the run. A value
of zero means the raw result was at or below the declared floor. A value of one means it was
at or above the declared ceiling.

This transformation does not make two tasks commensurate. Tracking and Pong still have
different observations, actions, opportunities, floors, and uncertainty.

## Anchors and provenance

`ScoreAnchor` records a value, kind, and provenance note. The kinds are:

- `ANALYTIC` for a mathematical bound or reference value;
- `NULL_MEASURED` for a measured null policy;
- `REFERENCE_MEASURED` for a measured reference agent.

Inspect the live task descriptor:

```julia
task = task_spec(DEFAULT_REGISTRY, :pong)
task.floor
task.ceiling
```

A measured null floor answers a narrow question. For example, `:null_random` estimates the
performance of random effectors under the declared body, task, horizon, and randomisation.
It is not a universal chance level.

`calibrate_task` measures anchors over declared seeds and stores provenance. Recalibrate
measured anchors when task dynamics, body semantics, the null policy, or the pinned runtime
changes.

## Gates

Some raw indicators admit a degenerate solution. A gate makes the missing condition
explicit.

For example, collision avoidance can be maximised by remaining still. A navigation task can
therefore require movement before its collision-free score counts. The gate is part of that
task's outcome definition. It is not a general measure of viability or competence.

Report the gate, its threshold, and the ungated descriptor when a reader needs to understand
why a run received its score.

## Descriptors

A task can declare `descriptor_keys` beside one `score_key`. Descriptors explain the run but
do not become additional task outcomes.

Examples include collision count, distance moved, no-op fraction, polarisation, and milling.
Do not choose a favourable descriptor after the run and present it as the primary result.

## Comparisons

For a within-task comparison, report:

- the outcome key;
- raw values and uncertainty;
- normalised values when they aid interpretation;
- floor and ceiling kinds with provenance;
- blocks, trials, horizon, warm-up, and seed policy;
- any gate and descriptor used to diagnose it;
- paired contrasts when conditions share randomised blocks.

Keep raw task results separate across tasks. A cross-task summary requires a declared
weighting and a reason that the aggregation answers the research question.
