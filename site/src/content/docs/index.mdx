## Two ways in

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Cellular cognition" icon="seti:julia">
    What can a single node design do? Test — and **evolve** — new neuron models
    within a strict locality constraint, and look for general self-organising
    competence. You **benchmark** node designs across tasks.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Collective intelligence" icon="puzzle">
    What dynamics emerge in the collective — read both as a **network**
    (nodes-in-a-reservoir) and a **multi-agent system** (agents-in-an-ensemble)?
    You **profile** and **sweep** to map the dynamics.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

The two meet in the **cross-level analysis**: the same dynamical signatures —
criticality, order, information flow — measured at the node scale and the agent
scale, and asked whether they covary.

:::note[Reservoir-computing *spirit*, not classical reservoir computing]
These are recurrent spiking node-networks read out by simple effectors — but the
Falandays weights **learn online**, the compartmental weights are **evolved**, and
there is **no trained linear readout**. The name is a nod, not a claim.
:::
