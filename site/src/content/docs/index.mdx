## An experimentation platform

BrainlessLab is a runnable lab for **minimal neural collectives**. Each collective contains
simple, homeostatic, neuron-like nodes. It has no trained readout and no controller. It is for
researchers who study real neurons or cells and want a small, designed collective they can run.

The anchor question is why the Falandays node works. You can test that question, design
variations, and compare them. A task score operationalizes performance under one task
contract. A capacity claim requires appropriate controls, construct validity, and
generalization; the score alone does not establish it or rank designs absolutely. These
tests span the cellular cognition of a reservoir and the collective behavior of a swarm.

The current [moving-shoal experiment](/experiments/shoal-vision-sweep/) makes that nested
programme concrete: canonical Falandays reservoirs control moving bodies, local perception
writes a changing inter-agent graph, and a matched sight-range sweep compares need
satisfaction, physical cohesion, coordinated movement, and perceptual connectivity. The
task, embodiment, and study remain explicitly Experimental even though the node is the
validated reference implementation.

The collective self-organises toward its own viability, not toward our tasks. BrainlessLab
measures whether the collective can be made to perform a task.

You can add tasks and environments. One generic [`Embodiment`](/receptors-effectors/)
composes geometry, sensors, encoders, actuators, dynamics, and physiology with stable ports;
[`ObjectWorld`](/environments-tasks/) composes objects, fields, appearance, and typed effects.
Biological and robotic organisms use the same public structure with different components.
The compute core is low-code and headless. It runs on a laptop and does not require a GPU.
The browser demo needs no installation; repository guidance lets a coding agent operate the
research tools for people who do not write Julia.

:::note[Ships with two agent skills]
The repository includes Julia and BrainlessLab skills plus a root `AGENTS.md`. Together they
teach a compatible coding agent the public architecture, verification gates, and scientific
boundaries instead of relying on a hidden prompt.
:::
