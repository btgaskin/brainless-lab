The analytic suite reads the *statistics of self-organised dynamics* at three scales.
Every measure below carries its equation, a one-line reading, and a **Source** callout
to its Julia implementation. All operate on channels recorded by the `Recorder` (off
the compute hot path), so nothing here perturbs a run.

## Criticality (node scale)

### Branching ratio — the MR estimator

For a population-activity series $A(t)$, the branching ratio $m$ is how much activity
one tick begets the next. The naive slope $\hat m = \sum A_t A_{t+1} / \sum A_t^2$ is
**biased under subsampling** (Wilting & Priesemann 2018) — and this is subsampling (a
scalar summary of the population). The **multistep-regression** estimator fixes it: for
lags $k = 1\dots k_{\max}$ take the (intercept-corrected) slope $r_k$ of $A(t{+}k)$ on
$A(t)$, then fit

$$
r_k = b\,m^{k} \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \log r_k = \log b + k\log m,
$$

and read $m$ off the exponential decay — which is invariant to the subsampling that
distorts the single-lag slope.

<p class="bl-source">Source: <code>src/analysis/Branching.jl</code> — <code>branching_ratio</code> (per-tick + legacy σ), <code>branching_ratio_mr</code>.</p>

:::caution
The Falandays homeostat regulates the population rate directly, so $m \approx 1$ can be
*rate-pinned* rather than emergent — read it alongside the spectral radius (below).
:::

### Neuronal avalanches

Threshold $A(t)$ into supra-threshold excursions; each avalanche has a **size** $S$
(summed activity) and **duration** $D$. Near criticality both are power laws,
$P(S)\sim S^{-\tau}$ and $P(D)\sim D^{-\alpha}$, tied by the **crackling-noise scaling
relation**

$$
\frac{\alpha-1}{\tau-1} \;=\; \gamma, \qquad \langle S\rangle(D)\sim D^{\gamma}.
$$

Agreement of the three exponents is a far stronger criticality claim than any single
$\sigma$ (Beggs & Plenz 2003).

<p class="bl-source">Source: <code>src/analysis/Avalanches.jl</code> — <code>avalanches</code> (τ, α, γ_fit vs γ_pred).</p>

### Spectral radius

The dominant magnitude of the learned recurrent matrix, $\rho(W) = \max_i |\lambda_i(W)|$
— the linear-stability read that complements the branching (avalanche) read. Tracked
over the rollout, and per agent.

<p class="bl-source">Source: <code>src/analysis/Spectral.jl</code>.</p>

### Per-node distance-to-target

Each node has a homeostatic target $T_i$; its instantaneous strain is $|a_i - T_i|$.
The across-node distribution is an "internal effort map" — which nodes are working,
which are settled.

<p class="bl-source">Source: <code>src/analysis/TargetError.jl</code> — <code>node_target_error</code>.</p>

## Cross-level — the same measure at two scales

The point of the whole programme: measure the *same* signature at the **node** scale and
the **agent** scale, and ask whether they covary. Every criticality estimator takes a
`level`:

- `:node` — within each agent's own reservoir (its node spikes),
- `:agent` — the **per-tick count of agents undergoing a turn event** ($|\Delta\theta| > \theta_{\text{thr}}$, default $\pi/12$) — a genuine agent-scale cascade, *not* the pooled node rate rescaled,
- `:pooled` — all nodes together (the default).

Because `:agent` is a distinct process, `branching_ratio(sim; level=:agent)` and
`level=:node` are comparable quantities you can plot against each other.

<p class="bl-source">Source: <code>src/analysis/ActivityLevels.jl</code>; consumed by Branching / Avalanches / SecondOrder.</p>

### Second-order signatures (both levels)

$$
\chi = N\,\mathrm{Var}[\phi], \qquad
F = \frac{\mathrm{Var}[A]}{\langle A\rangle}, \qquad
\mathrm{PR} = \frac{(\sum_i \lambda_i)^2}{\sum_i \lambda_i^2}
$$

**Susceptibility** $\chi$ (order parameter $\phi$ = synchrony at node scale, polarization
at agent scale), the **Fano factor** $F$, and the **participation ratio** PR (effective
dimensionality of the activity covariance). A criticality hypothesis predicts these peak
together across levels as a control parameter is swept.

<p class="bl-source">Source: <code>src/analysis/SecondOrder.jl</code>.</p>

## Collective (agent scale)

- **Polarization** $\;P = \big|\frac{1}{N}\sum_i e^{i\theta_i}\big|$ — heading alignment, $0$ disordered → $1$ aligned.
- **Milling** — rotational order about the torus-aware circular centroid.
- **Cohesion** — mean nearest-neighbour + pairwise distance (periodic-boundary aware).
- **Swarm regime** — a discrete label {polarized, milling, swarming, static} from $P$, milling, and speed.
- **Correlation length** — the distance at which the velocity-*fluctuation* correlation crosses zero (Cavagna et al. 2010 form: subtract the global mean velocity, normalize once globally).

<p class="bl-source">Source: <code>src/world/Metrics.jl</code> (polarization, milling, cohesion) and <code>src/analysis/SwarmAnalysis.jl</code> (<code>swarm_regime</code>, <code>correlation_length</code>).</p>

## Information flow

**Transfer entropy** — directional, order-1 discrete plug-in estimator:

$$
\mathrm{TE}_{X\to Y} = \sum p(y_{t+1}, y_t, x_t)\,\log_2\frac{p(y_{t+1}\mid y_t, x_t)}{p(y_{t+1}\mid y_t)} .
$$

Two variants: node-to-node **within a reservoir** (spike trains), and agent-to-agent
(binarised heading change) — the direct measure for the social-foraging "do they use
each other as a guide?" question. *Experimental* (plug-in bias; a KSG upgrade is flagged).

<p class="bl-source">Source: <code>src/analysis/TransferEntropy.jl</code> — <code>transfer_entropy</code>, <code>node_transfer_entropy</code>, <code>agent_transfer_entropy</code>.</p>
