## The problem it addresses

The **basal cognition** debate (a strand of the Diverse Intelligence program) is stuck on a
definitional deadlock: proponents ascribe "learning", "memory", and "decision-making" to cells,
tissues, and molecular networks; skeptics charge that these are metaphorical dilutions of concepts
whose semantics were fixed for brains. Chis-Ciure & Levin try to dissolve the deadlock by replacing
the umbrella concept "cognition" with a single, substrate-agnostic, **measurable** quantity:
**how efficiently a system searches its problem space** relative to blind chance.

## A lexicon for problem spaces

They extend Newell & Simon's (1972) symbolic problem-solving into a scale-agnostic quintuple:

$$
P = \langle S, O, C, E, H\rangle
$$

- **$S$** — physically realisable **states** (incl. initial $S_{\text{init}}$ and goal $S_{\text{goal}}$).
- **$O$** — **operators**, elementary transitions $o : S \to S$, each costed by a weight $w : O \to \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}$ (in physical units — Joules, ATP).
- **$C$** — **constraints**, forbidden state–operator pairs (the nomologically possible paths).
- **$E$** — an **evaluation** functional $S \to \mathbb{R}$ (a fitness proxy; in a variational reading, free energy).
- **$H$** — the **horizon**, the forward look-ahead depth (set by intrinsic timescales / delay lines).

Constraints, evaluation, and horizon are promoted to first-class elements because biological agents
**modulate them directly** — relaxing a constraint or re-tiling the landscape is itself a fingerprint
of intelligence, not just navigation within a fixed space.

## Intelligence as search efficiency

Intelligence (in William James's sense — a fixed goal reached by variable means) is operationalised as
a scalar. Let $\tau_{\text{blind}}$ be the expected cost of a **maximal-entropy random walk** on the
admissible graph to reach a goal, and $\tau_{\text{agent}}$ the cost under the agent's policy. Then:

$$
K = \log_{10}\!\left(\frac{\tau_{\text{blind}}}{\tau_{\text{agent}}}\right)
$$

$K$ counts **orders of magnitude of dissipative work saved** relative to chance. $K=0$ is chance;
$K=n$ is $10^{n}$-fold more efficient. Because both numerator and denominator scale with the
combinatorial size of $P$, $K$ is **scale-invariant** and **normalised for task difficulty**; it is
in **physical work units**, and it is **additive** across conditionally independent sub-problems
($K_{\text{total}} = \sum_j K_j$), hence compositional across nested scales. Crucially, $K$ is only as
good as its null model — an unfairly handicapped $\tau_{\text{blind}}$ inflates it — so the random walk
must run in the *exact same* $P$.

## Two worked estimates

| System | Problem space | $K$ | Reading |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| *Dictyostelium* chemotaxis | cortical-patch occupancy under a cAMP gradient | $\approx 2.2$ | ~150–200× faster than a random walk (≈7 bits of path-information) |
| Planarian head regeneration (BaCl₂) | ~2,700-gene expression manifold | $\approx 21$ | finds a ~10-gene resistance solution in 37 days vs an astronomical blind search ($10^{21}$×) |

Both are computed against **deliberately conservative** (intelligence-underestimating) null models, so
they are lower bounds. Even "simple" organisms prune combinatorial explosions by hundreds- to
sextillion-fold.

## The claim

The "mark of the cognitive" is best sought not in neurons but in the **measurable efficiency** with
which living systems traverse energy and information gradients to tame combinatorial explosions —
"one problem space at a time." The additive decomposition of $K$ across nested scales pinpoints
*where* and *by how much* intelligence condenses, rendering the "obvious" superiority over chance
measurable and therefore refutable. The account connects downstream to the Free Energy Principle
(with $E$ as variational free energy and optimal trajectories as least-action flows) and to
algorithmic-complexity / minimum-description-length notions of efficient computation.

:::tip[Why it's collected here]
Two ideas resonate with this lab's design at the conceptual level (not the specifics of any one task):
the **multi-scale competency** picture — nested agents each searching their own space — is the same
"reservoir within an ensemble" nesting the harness is built around; and the insistence that an
efficiency claim is **only as good as its explicit null model** is exactly the discipline the
