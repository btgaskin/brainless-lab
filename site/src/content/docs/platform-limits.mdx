---
title: Platform limits
description: What BrainlessLab currently models, what remains simplified or experimental, and which claims the platform cannot establish alone.
---

BrainlessLab is a laboratory for a particular question: what behavior and dynamical
signatures can arise when self-organising, neuron-like reservoirs are closed through bodies
and tasks without a trained readout or hand-written controller? It is not a general robotics
simulator, neuroscience model, benchmark authority, or proof that a behavior is cognitive.

## Scientific scope

The core object is a self-organising neural substrate and its closed sensorimotor loop.
Tasks operationalize behavior; analyses describe internal or collective dynamics; sweeps,
ablations, and controls test how those observations change.

The platform can support evidence about an explicitly implemented mechanism under declared
simulation conditions. It cannot by itself establish:

- biological fidelity beyond the validated implementation boundary;
- cognition, intelligence, autonomy, or motivation as natural kinds;
- transfer to animals, physical robots, or unmodeled environments;
- optimality outside the searched parameter and task region;
- causal necessity without an appropriate intervention and control;
- equivalence from a nonsignificant difference;
- criticality from a power law or one scalar estimator.

## Baseline fidelity

Fixture parity for `:falandays` validates the Julia node update against the local
authors-derived reference construction on tested fixtures. It does not validate every body,
task, experimental Falandays variant, ecological layer, or interpretation of the paper. The
package's additional variants and platform components are its own experimental work.
`:falandays_base` remains a compatibility alias.

## Physical and ecological limits

The generic `ObjectWorld` currently provides:

- two-dimensional toroidal or walled arenas in simulation units;
- fixed agent populations;
- static circular objects;
- named analytic scalar fields;
- simplified spectral appearance and illumination;
- typed contact or exposure effects;
- one compatible actuator/dynamics command path per assembled body.

It does not currently provide general meshes, rigid-body collision physics, fluid dynamics,
moving non-agent objects, births, replacement, lineage scheduling, or structural evolution
of the component graph. Death can make an existing agent inactive; it is not a population
turnover system.

Body presets named insect, robot, or UAV are reusable compositions, not high-fidelity models
of a species or vehicle. Sensor geometry, noise, actuator limits, and environmental fields
remain experimental assumptions that must be calibrated for each question.

## Sensing limits

Field probes sample explicitly supplied analytic fields. Object banks do not automatically
emit gradients. Spectral cameras use a simplified channel basis and illuminant; they do not
model a complete optical pipeline, wavelength-continuous material reflectance, diffraction,
or atmospheric effects. Their ray casts do implement simplified geometric occlusion.
Observations and action spaces remain task-designed.

`SectorVision` uses hard angular bins, a finite field of view, a configurable power-shaped
distance activation (linear by default), a configurable output gain, and only the nearest
target in each sector. Its bearing-sham control preserves the sampled
sector values while circularly shifting their alignment; it does not reproduce every
possible matched social null. The moving-shoal embodiment has no fluid forces, fin mechanics,
optic-flow stabilization, body-body collision response, or species-specific vision. Calling
it fish-like describes a compact moving visual-agent assay, not zoological fidelity.

## Evolution and development limits

`DevelopmentSpec` varies bounded scalar paths on a fixed component graph. It does not add or
remove components, evolve arbitrary topology, encode runtime state, or schedule
developmental events. The included optimizer is experimental infrastructure, and one
optimization run does not characterize search variance.

## Analysis limits

Finite runs and finite populations constrain every estimator. In particular:

- agent-scale statistics need enough independent agents and worlds;
- avalanche fits need many events, threshold/bin sensitivity, competing-distribution checks,
  and finite-size evidence;
- spectral radius is a linear proxy, not a general nonlinear stability criterion;
- transfer entropy needs adequate samples, stationarity checks, surrogate tests, and
  multiple-pair correction;
- shared input can mimic collective coupling;
- dead, failed, or saturated runs require declared handling.

The analysis pages label current caveats, but each new task and sampling regime still needs
its own validation.

## Software and workflow limits

The public interfaces are intended to be generic, but the physical, heterogeneous, and
ecological layers remain experimental. The current platform has fixed membership and no
browser-based protocol builder, automatic preregistration, sealed-data service, or automated
evidence-promotion command. Reproducibility means a traceable rerun in a declared environment;
bit-for-bit guarantees apply only where explicit fixture tests establish them.

Direct `Ensemble` simulation supports heterogeneous body and reservoir groups. Current
TOML/config-driven optimization and several high-level builders still require one fixed
receptor/effector layout for the population being materialized; they do not yet evolve
variable port structure.
