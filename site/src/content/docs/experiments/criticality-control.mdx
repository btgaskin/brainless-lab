---
title: Criticality is not the control variable
description: Does the Falandays homeostat tune the reservoir to criticality, and is that what makes it work? We interrogate the target mechanism directly and null-test the collective — the branching ratio near 1 is a byproduct, not the controlled variable.
---

**Question.** The [Falandays](/nodes/falandays/) homeostatic rule holds each reservoir's branching
ratio $m$ near $1$ — the textbook signature of self-organized criticality. Two things follow that
this page tests directly. First, at the **node**: is $m$ the *controlled* variable, and is function
driven by proximity to $m \approx 1$? Second, at the **collective**: is the swarm's flat
$m \approx 0.97$ *real* cross-agent criticality? The answer to both is **no**. Function is
controlled by the activity **rate** — held off saturation and silence by the *weight* rule — and
$m \approx 1$ is an **ambiguous byproduct**: it marks the healthy plateau, saturation, and silent
collapse alike, and at the collective it does not beat a circular-shift null.

**Design.** Two probes, at the two scales. **Experiment A** interrogates the target mechanism on a
single agent — a one-at-a-time sweep of the four homeostatic knobs on `:wall`, reading function
(nav), activity (rate), and criticality ($m$) together. **Experiment D** null-tests the collective —
the 48-agent forage swarm's branching ratio against a circular-shift surrogate that destroys
inter-agent timing. This is the evidence behind the [social-foraging](/experiments/social-foraging/)
page's open hypothesis of "**function without criticality**."

:::note[The honest posture]
This is a *dissection*, not a leaderboard. We are not asking how high $m$ climbs or how good the
score gets — we are asking **which actuator regulates what**, and whether the branching ratio near
$1$ is a tuned target, a driver of function, or neither. It turns out to be neither. Where a claim
is a spot-check rather than a promoted study, it is flagged as such.
:::

## The rule — two actuators, one error

Each node runs the **allostatic** Falandays reservoir (full update and learning equations in
[Nodes → Falandays](/nodes/falandays/); we do not re-derive them here). The error is the post-spike
distance from set-point, $e = \text{acts} - \text{target}$, and **two homeostatic actuators share
that one error**:

- **The weight rule.** Eligible weights — those whose presynaptic node fired last tick — move
  *opposite* the error, $w \mathrel{-}= (e / \text{counts}) \cdot \texttt{lrate\_wmat}$. This is the
  fast, per-synapse corrector.
- **The target rule.** The set-point itself drifts *toward* activity,
  $\text{target} \mathrel{+}= e \cdot \texttt{lrate\_targ}$, and is **floored** at `targ_min`. The
  spike threshold is $\text{target} \cdot \texttt{threshold\_mult}$.

So the target is an **allostatic** (moving, floored) set-point, not a fixed one. The whole question
of this page is *which of these two actuators does the real regulation, and of what.* Experiment A
answers it by weakening each in turn.

## Experiment A — the target-setup sweep

**Function is a plateau in the *rate*, not a peak at a criticality value.** Substrate `:wall`,
`node=:falandays_base`, single agent, **10 seeds, $T = 2000$**. We measure three things at once:
**nav** = normalized wall score (function — note it *ceilings at 1.0*, because wall is easy, so the
**failures** are the signal, not the ceiling), **rate** = mean firing rate (activity), and **$m$** =
pooled MR (multistep-regression) branching ratio (criticality). One knob moves at a time from the
default; `both` = freeze target *and* weights.

Four findings, each read straight off the table:

- **(i) Function is a plateau, and it breaks only at rate-regime failures.** nav sits at $\approx 1$
  across the *entire* healthy band — $m$ anywhere from $0.92$ to $0.99$, rate anywhere from $0.29$ to
  $0.79$. It collapses in exactly two places, both **rate** failures: **saturation** (`threshold_mult`
  $< 1.75 \Rightarrow$ rate $\to 1$, nav $\to 0.52$) and **silent collapse** (`lrate_targ` $\ge 0.1$
  or `lrate_wmat` $= 0.1 \Rightarrow$ rate $\to 0$, nav $\to 0$). The healthy plateau spans a wide
  band of $m$; the failures are read off the rate.
- **(ii) The weight rule is the load-bearing homeostat — not the target.** *Freezing the target*
  entirely (`lrate_targ` $= 0$) leaves function **perfect** (nav $1.000$, and $m$ actually *higher*
  at $0.990$). But *weakening or killing the weight rule* saturates and dies: `lrate_wmat` $= 0.1$
  under-corrects to silence (nav $0.000$), and freeze-both saturates to rate $1.000$ (nav $0.539$).
  The weight rule has a **Goldilocks strength** — $0.5$–$1.0$ works, $2.0$ over-corrects (nav
  $0.730$, rate $0.496$). The target can be switched off with no loss; the weight rule cannot.
- **(iii) The target sets the operating rate, and has a hard stability limit.** `targ_min` and
  `threshold_mult` slide the rate/regime smoothly ($m$ tracking as a byproduct — e.g. `targ_min`
  $0.1 \to 4.0$ walks rate $0.29 \to 0.39$ and $m$ $0.976 \to 0.923$). But driving the set-point
  *too fast* — `lrate_targ` $\ge 0.1$ — **destabilizes the loop into collapse** (rate $\to 0$, nav
  $\to 0$). The target is a slow governor, not a fast corrector.
- **(iv) $m$ is not the controlled variable.** It sits between $0.92$ and $0.99$ whenever the
  controller works *at all*, and — decisively — it is **ambiguous**: $m \approx 0.99$ shows up at the
  healthy plateau (`lrate_targ` $= 0$), at full saturation (`threshold_mult` $= 0.5$, $m = 0.991$,
  broken), and at dead silence (`threshold_mult` $= 3.0$, $m = 0.995$, nav $0.000$) alike. A number
  that is the *same* at the working point, at saturation, and at silence is not a control target.

as of the working regime.*

## Experiment D — the collective null-test

**The swarm's branching ratio near 1 is a pinned single-agent statistic, not genuine cross-agent
criticality.** Substrate `:forage`, `node=:falandays_oosawa`, 48 agents, social, $T = 3000$. Here
$m_\text{agent}$ is the MR branching ratio on the `:align` collective activity; **activity** =
$\overline{\text{agent\_activity}}/N$ (a value $\to 1$ would be saturation); and the **crossshift
null** is a circular-shift surrogate that destroys inter-agent timing while preserving each agent's
own statistics — so its ratio $= \text{real}/\text{null\_mean} \approx 1$ means *no real cross-agent
structure*. Representative cells across conspecific-gain (cg) and vision-range (vr):

| cg | vr | m_agent | activity | null real/mean → ratio |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1.5 | 0.977 | 0.122 | 0.982 / 0.921 → **1.07** |
| 4 | 4.0 | 0.977 | 0.150 | 0.977 / 0.943 → **1.04** |
| 4 | 6.0 | 0.969 | 0.150 | 0.977 / 0.955 → **1.02** |
| 16 | 4.0 | 0.963 | 0.150 | 0.969 / 0.958 → **1.01** |
| 2 | 2.5 | 0.954 | 0.149 | 0.975 / 0.923 → **1.06** |

Three things line up, and they rule out the two innocent explanations for a flat $m \approx 1$:

- **$m_\text{agent}$ is flat $\approx 0.95$–$0.98$** across the cells — exactly as the
  [social-foraging](/experiments/social-foraging/) landscape reported, and exactly what an
  allostatic rate-pin predicts.
- **Activity is only $\approx 0.12$–$0.15$ — nowhere near saturation.** So the flat $m$ is *not* the
  saturation artifact (a network firing flat-out also reads $m \to 1$); this swarm is firing
  sparsely and still shows $m \approx 0.97$.
- **The crossshift-null ratio is $\approx 1.0$** ($1.01$–$1.07$). $m_\text{agent}$ barely exceeds a
  surrogate whose inter-agent timing has been shuffled away — so essentially *all* of the branching
  signal survives destroying the cross-agent structure. The $m \approx 0.97$ is therefore a **pinned
  single-agent statistic** riding on 48 independently rate-regulated reservoirs, **not** a collective
  critical avalanche.

:::note[This is a spot-check]
Experiment D is **5 representative cells**, with **3 seeds** for $m$/activity and **1 seed $\times$
30 shifts** for each null — a targeted spot-check chosen to break the saturation and cross-structure
alternatives, not the full grid. It should be scaled (20+ seeds, the whole cg $\times$ vr landscape)
before promotion to a run-dir study. Read it as *decisive against* genuine collective criticality on
these cells, not as a complete map.
:::

## Synthesis — a byproduct, and an ambiguous one

**The branching ratio near $1$ is neither tuned criticality nor the driver of function — at the node
it is a byproduct of rate homeostasis carried by the *weight* rule, and it is ambiguous; at the
collective it does not beat a null.**

Pulling the two probes together:

- **What actually governs function is the activity rate.** Experiment A's plateau breaks *only* at the
  two rate-regime failures — saturation and silence — and both are held off by the **weight** rule
  (freezing the *target* costs nothing; weakening the *weight* rule kills the network). $m$ is a
  passenger: it slides as a byproduct when the rate slides, and it is *identical* at the working
  point, at saturation, and at silence.
- **So $\sigma_\text{eff}$ / branching $\to 1$ must always be read *beside* the rate.** This is exactly
  the caveat that shadows [Bessone & Plantec's](/notes/emergent-macro-criticality/) effective
  branching ratio: their $\sigma_\text{eff}$ reaches $1$ in the **saturated** phase as readily as at
  the critical band, so $\sigma_\text{eff} \to 1$ alone cannot certify criticality — the rate (or the
  avalanche-shape exponents) has to disambiguate. Our figure is the single-agent version of that same
  ambiguity, drawn explicitly.
- **It is also why branching and spectral radius decouple here.** Per
  [Larremore, Shew & Restrepo](/notes/spectral-radius-criticality/), the branching criterion
  $\sigma = 1$ is only the *homogeneous* special case of the topology-general critical point
  $\lambda(W) = 1$. The allostatic homeostat pins the *rate-driven branching statistic* to $1$
  regardless — but that pinning rides on rate control, not on the weight loop actually sitting at the
  spectral critical point, so the two "signatures" need not move together (as the
  [tracking sweep](/experiments/tracking-param-sweep/) already found $m$ rate-pinned while $\rho(W)$
  only flags boundedness).
- **At the collective, connectivity and cohesion govern function, not criticality.** The
  [social-foraging](/experiments/social-foraging/) page showed transmission climbing with cohesion
  and interaction-graph connectivity — *above* the percolation threshold — while $m_\text{agent}$
  stayed flat. Experiment D closes that loop: the flat $m$ is not real collective criticality (it
  fails the crossshift null), so there is nothing for function to be "displaced from."

This is the mechanism behind **function without criticality**: in this *designed* homeostatic system,
$m \approx 1$ is a rate-homeostasis byproduct — the same value at health, saturation, and silence —
not the tuned target and not the driver of what the network does.

## Reproduce it

**Experiment A (node).** One cell per row of the table — sweep a single knob, read function,
activity, and criticality off the same run:

```julia
using BrainlessLab, Statistics

s = 1
sim = simulate(:wall; node=:falandays_base,
    lrate_targ=0.0, targ_min=1.0, threshold_mult=2.0, lrate_wmat=1.0,  # move one at a time
    ticks=2000, seed=s)

nav  = sim.metrics.nav_score                              # function (ceilings at 1.0 on :wall)
rate = sim.metrics.rate_mean                              # activity
m    = branching_ratio_mr(sim; level=:pooled).m_mr        # criticality (byproduct)
```

The table rows are the mean over 10 seeds; `both` freezes target *and* weights
(`lrate_targ=0.0, lrate_wmat=0.0`).

**Experiment D (collective).** Run the social forage config, then measure the swarm branching ratio
and its circular-shift null on the same rollout:

```julia
using BrainlessLab, Statistics, Random

vr = 4.0; N = 48; s = 1
sim = simulate(:forage; node=:falandays_oosawa, n_agents=N, n_nodes=100,
    space_size=12.0, vision_range=vr, source_vision_range=0.8,
    conspecific_gain=4.0, source_gain=2.0, noise_gain=0.25,
    motor=KinematicMotor(top_speed=0.1),
    visual_coupling=true, conspecific_vision=true,
    ticks=3000, seed=s)

r        = branching_ratio_mr(sim; level=:agent, observable=(kind=:align, neighbor_radius=vr))
m_agent  = r.m_mr
activity = mean(r.agent_activity) / N                     # ≈0.12–0.15 here — not saturated

ratio = crossshift_null(sim,
    sm -> branching_ratio_mr(sm; level=:agent,
              observable=(kind=:align, neighbor_radius=vr)).m_mr;
    n_shifts=30, rng=MersenneTwister(s)).ratio            # ≈1.0 ⇒ no cross-agent structure
```

:::note[Provenance]
Both experiments are **exploratory scripts** — a handful of seeds and ad-hoc harnesses — and are
**not yet promoted** to the `experiments/` run-dir framework (`results.json` + `manifest.txt`, git
SHA, seed lists) that backs the [tracking sweep](/experiments/tracking-param-sweep/). They dissect a
mechanism and demonstrate the null-test method; the finalized study (full grid, 20+ seeds, the whole
crossshift battery) is the natural next step.
:::

## Open questions

:::note[The honest posture]
None of this says criticality is irrelevant *in general*. It says that in **this designed
homeostatic system**, the branching ratio near $1$ is a byproduct of rate control, not the tuned
target and not the driver of function. That is a claim about *this* instrument, deliberately kept
narrow.
:::

1. **Experiment A is on `:wall` base.** Other tasks sit at different $m$ — tracking is rate-pinned at
   $m \approx 0.99$ (see the [tracking sweep](/experiments/tracking-param-sweep/)). The *ambiguity*
   of $m$ (health / saturation / silence sharing a value) generalizes, but the exact numbers in the
   table are task-specific.
2. **`nav` ceilings at $1.0$ on wall.** The plateau reads as "robustly solves it," so the ceiling
   carries no information — the **failures** (saturation, collapse) are the informative part, and a
   harder task with head-room would sharpen the plateau's edges.
3. **The collective null is a spot-check.** 5 cells, 3 seeds for $m$/activity, 1 seed $\times$ 30
   shifts for each null. It is decisive *against* collective criticality on those cells, but the full
   cg $\times$ vr grid with more seeds should be run before this is a promoted study.
4. **"Byproduct" is not "irrelevant."** The branching ratio near $1$ is still a genuine, reproducible
   feature of the healthy regime — it is simply *downstream* of rate homeostasis, not upstream of
   function. Whether a task exists where the designed reservoir can be pushed *off* $m \approx 1$
   while still functioning — separating the two cleanly — is the sharpest open test.
