**Question.** The authors-faithful Falandays object-tracking reservoir self-organizes online. Over
its homeostatic parameters, *where* does a random-init reservoir clear a declared **development
viability threshold**, and what internal signature accompanies viable versus non-viable dynamics?

**Design.** A one-at-a-time sweep over six parameters (`leak`, `lrate_targ`, `lrate_wmat`,
`input_amp`, `movement_amp`, `eye_offset`) around the locked paper baseline, plus a
`leak × lrate_wmat` **factorial** — the two axes that interact through the homeostatic loop gain.
**100 random-init seeds** each (random start heading, object position, and weight init), 7200
ticks, 100-tick warmup.

```bash
julia -t auto --project=. experiments/run.jl tracking_param_sweep
julia -t auto --project=. experiments/run.jl tracking_leak_lrate_factorial
```

**Performance is chance-anchored.** The viability variable is **`frac_within_30deg`** — the
fraction of post-warmup ticks the gaze is within 30° of the target. Its chance floor (a random
gaze against a sweeping stimulus) is a clean **1/6 ≈ 0.167**; a seed is called
**development-viable** when it clears that floor with margin (> 0.25).

:::note[The honest posture]
The authors-faithful baseline is a **weak, unreliable tracker** — most random inits sit near chance
and a **minority "ignite"** and genuinely track. This is exactly why the object of study is the
*viability space* (where and how often it works), not a single score.
:::

## Viability space — an ignition mixture

At a working setting, performance over random inits is **not** a tight band around a mean — it is a
**chance-pinned failure mode plus a viable tail** whose mass grows across the parameter boundary.
Mean ± sd would average "some track, some die" into a misleading "mediocre," so each panel shows the
**full per-seed distribution** with the chance line drawn in.

![Per-seed fraction-within-30° for each parameter, with the chance floor and per-seed regime colouring](/experiments/tracking-sweep/viability_strips.png)

The recalibrated **fraction of development-viable inits** (with a bootstrap CI), per axis:

![Fraction of random inits that clear the development viability threshold, per parameter, with bootstrap CI](/experiments/tracking-sweep/frac_viable.png)

Reading the panels (viable fraction = share of 100 inits clearing the 0.25 bar):

- **Four parameters have their best observed value at the paper setting in this grid.** `input_amp` peaks sharply at
  **0.75** (viable fraction 0.67 — every other value is worse), `movement_amp` at **10** (0.67),
  `eye_offset` at **30°** (0.67, falling to 0.23 at 60°), and `lrate_targ` is essentially flat
  across 0.01–0.04 (≈0.7) — the paper's 0.01 is as good as any.
- **Two are not.** `leak` peaks at **0.5** (viable fraction 0.86, vs 0.67 at the paper's 0.25),
  viable across roughly [0.25, 0.75] and dead at 0 — a saturating, chance-pinned collapse.
  `lrate_wmat` peaks near **0.1** (0.72), viable across roughly [0.05, 1.0], and is **degenerate at
  both ends**: 0.01 (under-correcting) and 2.0 (over-correcting) both collapse to chance. The
  paper's 1.0 is viable but past the best observed single-axis cell.

## The joint landscape — leak × lrate_wmat interact

The two axes that move performance set the **homeostatic loop gain** together (leak is the
activation decay, `lrate_wmat` scales the weight correction), so the one-at-a-time marginals mislead
exactly here. The factorial maps the joint viable region:

![leak × lrate_wmat viability heatmap — fraction of inits tracking](/experiments/tracking-sweep/factorial_heatmap.png)

The exploratory factorial shows a structured **diagonal viable ridge**. `leak` and `lrate_wmat` are
*positively* coupled: more activation decay needs more aggressive weight correction to stay
regulated. Low leak is viable only at low `lrate_wmat`, high leak only at high `lrate_wmat`, and the
off-diagonal corners are **dead** (0.00 viable). The best observed joint cell is
**leak = 0.4, lrate_wmat = 1.0** (viable fraction **0.90**)—at the *paper's* learning
rate, not the best observed marginal `lrate_wmat` cell
of ~0.1. So the one-at-a-time marginals actively mislead here: the low-`lrate_wmat` region that
looks best at the baseline leak is dead once leak ≥ 0.4. The largest improvement observed over the
paper baseline is **leak 0.25 → 0.4**, holding everything else fixed.

## The signature — homeostatic set-point error, not branching

Two obvious "signatures" are compromised for this model, and understanding *why* is the point:

- **Branching ratio `m` is rate-pinned.** The homeostat's job is to hold each node near its target,
  so population rate is a *controlled* variable — `m ≈ 0.99` across the whole healthy interior is a
  tautology, not emergence. It moves only where control *fails*.
- **Spectral radius ρ(W)** flags the *boundedness* of the weight loop (it explodes when the learned
  weights diverge), but as a linear read of a threshold-and-reset spiking net it is a proxy, and it
  is flat across the non-degenerate interior. (See [Larremore et al. 2011](/notes/spectral-radius-criticality/)
  for ρ as a criticality control in threshold networks — a nuance, not a licence.)

The honest signature is **`node_target_error`** — `|acts − targets|`, the very quantity the homeostat
controls. Pooling every seed across every cell, its tail (p90) separates viable from non-viable inits:

![Per-seed node_target_error tail vs track_score, coloured by regime](/experiments/tracking-sweep/signature_scatter.png)

Pooled over 3,200 seed-by-cell observations, the relationship is **modest**—viable runs average a
set-point-error tail of 0.31 vs 0.39 for non-viable, and the correlation with `track_score` is
−0.16. The honest reading is *asymmetric*: in this development grid, a **high** set-point
error is associated with the non-viable cluster, while a **low** error is
**necessary, not sufficient** for tracking. So `node_target_error` earns its place as the most
*mechanistically motivated candidate*—native to the homeostat's own control objective, not an
imported criticality proxy—working as a development-set failure detector. These are repeated
seed-by-cell observations, not 3,200 independent seeds: parameter cells and seed labels
structure the pool. The stronger claim, that it separates ignited from dead inits at a fixed
parameter setting on untouched runs, requires a stratified or held-out confirmation.
