This experiment is a **gesture toward a cross-scale study**, not a completed fish model or a
confirmatory result. Sixteen independently initialized `:falandays` reservoirs control moving
agents in a walled arena. Each agent must maintain two material needs by encountering two
persistent sources. The sweep asks a deliberately narrow first question:

> As the range of conspecific sight changes, do material-need satisfaction and grouped
> movement change—and do those changes require correct social bearings or an active
> association need?

The primary endpoint is **mean material satisfaction**. Grouped movement is kept descriptive
and decomposed into physical cohesion and directional coherence; there is no single invented
“shoaling score.”

## Exact implemented loop

Every agent has the same 51-input, three-output interface:

| Bank | Width | Meaning |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| conspecific sectors | 16 | nearest visible agent per egocentric sector |
| source-one sectors | 16 | nearest visible source-one object per sector |
| source-two sectors | 16 | nearest visible source-two object per sector |
| physiology | 3 | resource one, resource two, and association state |

The sector banks cover a 300° field of view. By default they decrease linearly with surface
distance and have unit gain; both gain and the power applied to normalized proximity are
explicit task parameters.
Source sight remains fixed at 6 simulation units. Conspecific sight is swept over 2, 3.5, 5,
7, and 10 units. The outputs are `left_turn`, `right_turn`, and `thrust`. Antagonistic left and
right activation set turning; thrust continuously scales forward speed from zero to 0.2 units
per tick. Agents can therefore express different speeds, including stopping, directly from
reservoir activity rather than through a fixed gait.

The “fish” label is morphological shorthand. The body is a disc with unicycle dynamics; it
does not model fins, hydrodynamics, optic flow, occlusion by other bodies, or a particular
species. What it does preserve is the question made salient by fish work: motion and local
visual information can structure social attraction and alter information flow through a
moving group.

## Needs and controls

Resource-one and resource-two states drift downward by 0.001 per tick and recover by 0.01 on
contact with their corresponding source. Both sources are persistent and unlimited, so this
pilot studies alternating regulation rather than depletion and competition.

The association need, when active, has the same downward drift and receives a graded local
restoration within radius 2. Association restoration is computed from physical proximity,
not from the visual sensor. When association is off, its input port remains present but is
pinned at one; this preserves reservoir width across conditions.

The material and active association variables use a stochastic perturbation when below
setpoint: gain 1 with emission probability 0.2. This is **inspired by** the predictable versus
unpredictable feedback loop in [Kagan et al.'s embodied neuronal-culture
work](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2022.09.001); it is not a
reproduction of DishBrain stimulation or evidence that the simulated reservoirs implement
the same learning mechanism.

Three conspecific conditions separate visibility from directional information:

- **blind**: the conspecific bank is zero;
- **veridical**: occupied sectors retain their true egocentric bearings;
- **bearing sham**: the same instantaneous sector values are deterministically circularly
  shifted, preserving signal availability while breaking alignment to the neighbour's true
  bearing.

Each is crossed with association need on/off. Blind is run once per association setting and
block; veridical and bearing-sham conditions are run at every sight range. Initial agents,
headings, sources, and reservoirs are matched within a block.

## What is measured

The declared primary endpoint averages normalized satisfaction over both material needs,
agents, and post-warm-up samples. Secondary material outcomes include the lower of the two
need satisfactions and the fraction of agent-samples where both exceed 0.8.

Grouped movement has two distinct parts:

- **cohesion**: mean nearest-neighbour distance and the fraction of agents in the largest
  physical proximity component at surface-distance radius 2;
- **movement coherence**: `|sum(displacement)| / sum(|displacement|)` across agents, where
  zero means recorded displacements cancel and one means they are perfectly aligned.

The run also reconstructs the dynamic, directed perceptual graph from the agent poses and
sector geometry: mean out-degree, largest weak component, and consecutive-frame edge
turnover. These graph measures describe the social information opportunity created jointly
by vision and movement. They do not establish information transfer or criticality.

Wall occupancy is a required failure-mode diagnostic. A high movement-coherence value in a
wall-dominated run can reflect common boundary following rather than a cohesive moving
group; it must be read alongside nearest-neighbour distance and the physical proximity
component, never alone.

Contact rate, path length, speed, and coherence use the recorder grid. With the default
`record_every = 5`, contacts and chord-based path lengths are lower-bound diagnostics. Need
satisfaction is consequently the cleaner endpoint for this pilot.

When a screen changes material depletion, raw satisfaction is no longer on a common demand
scale. The analysis therefore also reports **material regulation gain**:

`(observed satisfaction - deterministic no-contact floor) / (1 - no-contact floor)`.

The no-contact floor is reconstructed at the actual recorder ticks from each need's initial
state and drift. This removes the mechanical effect of a faster or slower falling baseline.
It does not make depletion a pure scoring intervention: changing need dynamics also changes
the stochastic feedback presented to the reservoir.

## Operating-point sensitivity screen

The sight-range pilot is complemented by a bounded one-factor-at-a-time screen. It fixes
veridical conspecific sight at range 5, keeps the association need active, and runs one
baseline plus low/high values for 17 axes in each of two matched blocks:

- social and resource visual-input gain;
- social and resource distance-response exponent;
- material depletion, contact replenishment, and need-feedback gain, exponent, and emission
  probability;
- association depletion and feedback gain, exponent, and emission probability;
- association restoration amount, proximity radius, and target-neighbour normalization;
- resource sight range.

That is 70 jobs total. The point is to detect strong operating-point dependence before more
blocks are spent. It is not a factorial design, cannot estimate interactions, and does not
identify an optimum. Raw material satisfaction, demand-normalized material regulation,
association satisfaction, physical grouping, movement coherence, source contacts, speed,
and wall occupancy are kept as separate outputs.

This covers every **exposed operational** need control in the task. Need structure is held
fixed: minimum 0, initial/setpoint/maximum 1, below-setpoint deficit, Bernoulli feedback, and
no failure state. Those define the construct rather than a local operating point and are not
quietly folded into this screen.

## Relation to the motivating literature

The distance sweep deliberately echoes [Bessone and Plantec's static signalling
system](/notes/emergent-macro-criticality/), but the dependent variable is different. Their
agents occupy fixed positions, emit binary light, and are evaluated through cascade
statistics as internal propagation and spatial connectivity change. Here bodies move,
sources close a task loop, and the first question is performance and group organization.
This protocol makes **no criticality claim**.

[Lemasson et al. (2018)](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-018-27807-1) show that motion cues can
tune zebrafish directional decisions and social influence. [Ward, Kent, and Webster
(2020)](https://doi.org/10.3389/fevo.2020.00015) review how active attraction and passive
assortment by movement can both structure fish groups. Those studies motivate preserving
speed, bearing, and social attraction as separable variables. They do not biologically
validate this embodiment.

## Run the small pilot

```bash
julia -t 4 --project=. experiments/run.jl shoal_vision_sweep
julia -t 4 --project=. experiments/run.jl shoal_sensitivity_screen
```

The default keeps every condition but uses only two matched blocks, 1,500 ticks, and a
500-tick warm-up: 44 runs total. It writes one atomic result per condition, per-agent tables,
paired contrasts, figure inputs, resolved protocol, seed ledger, hashes, and status under
`experiments/runs/shoal_vision_sweep/`. Interrupted runs can resume from completed job files.

The only honest first reading is descriptive: curve shape, sign and scale of matched
differences, obvious degeneracy, and whether the pipeline produces interpretable variation.
No p-values, criticality claims, or “social intelligence” conclusion are authorized by this
pilot. A null or inconsistent result is valid and should guide simplification before more
blocks are spent.

The sensitivity command uses the same tick and warm-up budget for 70 one-factor jobs and
writes to `experiments/runs/shoal_sensitivity_screen/`. Completed job files are resume-safe;
the aggregate table and paired contrasts are regenerated after every complete invocation.
